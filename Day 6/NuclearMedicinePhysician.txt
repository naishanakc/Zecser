Job Summary 
A Nuclear Medicine Physician uses radioactive tracers (radiopharmaceuticals) to 
diagnose and treat diseases, especially cancer, thyroid disorders, and cardiac conditions. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Perform and interpret PET scans, SPECT scans, and nuclear imaging  
• Administer radiotracers safely  
• Diagnose cancers, thyroid disorders, and heart diseases  
• Conduct radionuclide therapy (e.g., thyroid cancer treatment)  
• Ensure radiation safety protocols  
• Work with oncologists, cardiologists, and endocrinologists  
Qualification & Education 
• MBBS  
• MD/DNB in Nuclear Medicine (or MD Radiology + specialization)  
Skills Required 
• Knowledge of radiation physics and safety  
• Expertise in functional imaging  
• Precision in handling radioactive materials  
• Analytical interpretation skills  
Work Settings 
• Nuclear medicine departments  
• Cancer hospitals  
• Advanced diagnostic centers  
Salary (India Estimate) 
• Entry Level: ₹20–35 LPA  
• Mid-Level: ₹35–70 LPA  
• Senior: ₹70 LPA – ₹1 Cr+  
Work Nature 
• Requires strict radiation safety practices  
• Highly specialized and technology-driven
