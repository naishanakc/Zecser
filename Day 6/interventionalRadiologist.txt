Job Summary 
An Interventional Radiologist (IR) performs minimally invasive, image-guided procedures 
to diagnose and treat diseases, often replacing traditional open surgery. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Perform image-guided procedures using CT, ultrasound, or fluoroscopy  
• Conduct biopsies, drainages, and catheter insertions  
• Treat tumors using ablation (radiofrequency, microwave)  
• Perform angiography and embolization procedures  
• Manage patient care before, during, and after procedures  
• Handle emergency interventions (bleeding control, trauma cases)  
Qualification & Education 
• MBBS  
• MD/DNB in Radiology  
• Fellowship/DM in Interventional Radiology  
Skills Required 
• Strong procedural and surgical skills  
• Hand-eye coordination  
• Knowledge of imaging and anatomy  
• Decision-making under pressure  
• Patient management and clinical skills  
Work Settings 
• Multispecialty hospitals  
• Trauma centers  
• Cancer institutes  
Salary (India Estimate) 
• Entry: ₹20–35 LPA  
• Mid-Level: ₹35–70 LPA  
• Senior: ₹70 LPA – ₹1.5 Cr+  
Work Nature 
• High-pressure, emergency-based  
• Involves radiation exposure (with safety protocols)  
• On-call duties common  
Career Growth 
Junior IR → Consultant IR → Senior Consultant → Head of Interventional Radiology
