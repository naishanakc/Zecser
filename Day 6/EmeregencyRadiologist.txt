Job Summary 
An Emergency Radiologist specializes in rapid interpretation of imaging studies in 
emergency settings, helping diagnose life-threatening conditions such as trauma, stroke, and 
internal bleeding. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Interpret urgent imaging (CT, X-ray, MRI, Ultrasound)  
• Diagnose trauma injuries (fractures, hemorrhage, organ damage)  
• Provide immediate reports for ER (Emergency Room) decisions  
• Work closely with emergency physicians and trauma surgeons  
• Prioritize critical cases and ensure quick turnaround time  
• Handle night shifts and high-volume workloads  
Qualification & Education 
• MBBS  
• MD/DNB in Radiology  
• Fellowship in Emergency Radiology (optional but preferred)  
Skills Required 
• Fast and accurate decision-making  
• Ability to work under pressure  
• Strong knowledge of trauma imaging  
• Excellent time management  
• Communication in critical situations  
Work Settings 
• Emergency departments (ER)  
• Trauma centers  
• Large tertiary hospitals  
Salary (India Estimate) 
• Entry Level: ₹15–25 LPA  
• Mid-Level: ₹25–50 LPA  
• Senior: ₹50–90+ LPA  
Work Nature 
• High stress, time-sensitive role  
• Night shifts and on-call duties common  
Career Growth 
Emergency Radiologist → Senior Emergency Specialist → Trauma Imaging Head
