Job Summary 
A Diagnostic Radiologist specializes in analyzing medical images to provide accurate and 
detailed diagnostic reports, often focusing on specific modalities or organ systems. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Perform and interpret diagnostic imaging studies  
• Specialize in modalities like CT, MRI, or Ultrasound  
• Detect diseases such as cancer, neurological disorders, and internal injuries  
• Collaborate with specialists (oncologists, neurologists, surgeons)  
• Maintain high accuracy in reporting  
• Ensure adherence to imaging protocols and quality standards  
Qualification & Education 
• MBBS  
• MD/DNB in Radiology  
• Fellowship (optional specialization – e.g., Neuro, MSK, Breast Imaging)  
Skills Required 
• Advanced imaging interpretation skills  
• Subspecialty knowledge (optional but valuable)  
• Precision and accuracy  
• Time management (high reporting workload)  
• Multidisciplinary collaboration  
Work Settings 
• Super-specialty hospitals  
• Diagnostic labs (e.g., MRI/CT centers)  
• Academic & research institutions  
Salary (India Estimate) 
• Entry Level: ₹10–18 LPA  
• Mid-Level: ₹20–40 LPA  
• Senior: ₹40–80+ LPA  
Career Growth 
Diagnostic Radiologist → Subspecialist Radiologist → Senior Consultant → Imaging
