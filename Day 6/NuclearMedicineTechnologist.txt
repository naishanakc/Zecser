Job Summary 
A Nuclear Medicine Technologist performs functional imaging procedures using 
radioactive materials to diagnose and treat diseases. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Prepare and administer radiotracers  
• Perform imaging procedures like SPECT and PET scans  
• Assist Nuclear Medicine Physicians in diagnosis and therapy  
• Ensure safe handling and disposal of radioactive materials  
• Monitor patients during procedures  
• Maintain regulatory and safety compliance  
Qualification 
• BSc in Nuclear Medicine Technology / Radiology  
• Certification in nuclear medicine  
Skills 
• Radiation physics knowledge  
• Safety compliance expertise  
• Technical imaging skills  
• Patient monitoring  
Work Settings 
• Nuclear medicine departments  
• Oncology centers  
• Research hospitals  
Salary 
₹4–12 LPA (can increase with experience) 
Career Growth 
Nuclear Medicine Technologist → Senior Technologist → Radiation Safety Officer → 
Department Head
