 Radiation Safety Officer (RSO) 
Job Summary 
A Radiation Safety Officer (RSO) is responsible for ensuring the safe use of radiation in 
medical imaging and protecting patients, staff, and the environment from radiation hazards. 
Key Responsibilities 
• Monitor and control radiation exposure levels  
• Ensure compliance with radiation safety regulations (e.g., AERB in India)  
• Conduct regular radiation safety audits  
• Maintain radiation records and reports  
• Train staff on radiation safety protocols  
• Oversee safe handling and disposal of radioactive materials  
• Investigate radiation incidents and implement corrective actions  
Qualification 
• MSc in Medical Physics / Radiological Physics  
• Certification/licensing from regulatory authority (e.g., AERB)  
Skills Required 
• Strong knowledge of radiation physics  
• Regulatory compliance expertise  
• Attention to detail  
• Risk assessment and safety management  
Work Settings 
• Hospitals  
• Nuclear medicine departments  
• Cancer treatment centers  
• Research institutions  
Salary (India) 
• Entry: ₹6–10 LPA  
• Mid-Level: ₹10–18 LPA  
• Senior: ₹18–30+ LPA  
Career Growth 
RSO → Senior RSO → Chief Radiation Safety Officer → Regulatory Consultant
